# Risk Management

Risk management is the process of identifying, assessing, and reducing cybersecurity risks that could impact systems, networks, or data.

Organizations use risk management to protect information and maintain secure operations.

---

# Common Risk Management Steps

1. Identify risks
2. Assess the impact of risks
3. Implement security controls
4. Monitor and review risks regularly

---

# Types of Cybersecurity Risks

## Malware
Malicious software designed to damage systems or steal data.

Examples:
- Viruses
- Ransomware
- Trojans

---

## Phishing
Fraudulent attempts to trick users into revealing sensitive information.

Examples:
- Fake emails
- Malicious links
- Fake login pages

---

## Weak Passwords
Poor password security can allow attackers unauthorized access.

Examples:
- Short passwords
- Reused passwords
- Predictable passwords

---

# Security Controls

Security controls are protections used to reduce cybersecurity risks.

## Examples
- Firewalls
- Antivirus software
- Encryption
- Access controls
- Employee security awareness training

---

# Why Risk Management Matters

Risk management helps organizations:
- reduce security threats,
- protect sensitive information,
- maintain business operations,
- and improve overall cybersecurity readiness.

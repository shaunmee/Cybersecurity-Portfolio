# CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is a foundational cybersecurity model used to guide security policies and protect information systems.

CIA stands for:
- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

---

# Confidentiality

Confidentiality ensures that sensitive information is only accessible to authorized users.

## Examples
- Passwords
- Encryption
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Access controls

## Cybersecurity Importance
Protects private and sensitive data from unauthorized access.

---

# Integrity

Integrity ensures that data remains accurate, complete, and unmodified unless authorized.

## Examples
- File hashing
- Backups
- Version control
- Digital signatures

## Cybersecurity Importance
Prevents unauthorized changes to systems and information.

---

# Availability

Availability ensures that systems and data are accessible when needed.

## Examples
- System backups
- Redundant systems
- Disaster recovery plans
- System maintenance

## Cybersecurity Importance
Helps organizations maintain reliable access to services and information.

---

# Why the CIA Triad Matters

The CIA Triad helps organizations:
- protect sensitive data,
- maintain trustworthy systems,
- and ensure critical services remain available.

It is one of the most important concepts in cybersecurity fundamentals.
